Understanding the Crisis

üìà Why Interest Rates Matter

Current Effective Rate: ~7.5%

The interest rate shown in this visualization (default 7.5%) represents the blended average rate the US government pays on its debt. This isn't a single rate‚Äîit's a weighted average across all Treasury securities (bills, notes, bonds) with different maturities and coupon rates.

‚ö†Ô∏è The Death Spiral Effect

As national debt grows relative to GDP, credit rating agencies (Moody's, S&P, Fitch) will downgrade US Treasury bonds. This has already begun:

  • 2011: S&P downgraded US from AAA to AA+
  • 2023: Fitch downgraded US from AAA to AA+
  • 2023: Moody's changed outlook to "negative"

Each downgrade forces the government to offer higher interest rates to attract buyers for new bonds. Higher rates mean:

  1. More money spent on interest payments
  2. Larger deficits ‚Üí more borrowing needed
  3. Debt grows faster ‚Üí further downgrades
  4. Even higher rates required ‚Üí REPEAT
